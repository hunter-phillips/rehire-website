Prompt

How does the RA role work to contribute to the Office of Residence Life & Student Conduct’s mission to provide “a safe, supportive community that encourages holistic growth”? How have you done this during the 2021-2022 Academic Year?

Introduction

The Office of Residence Life & Student Conduct's mission is to provide "a safe, supportive community that encourages holistic growth." A Resident Assistant's (RA) goal should be to implement and fulfill this mission through educational programs, meaningful conversations, and community building.

Educational Programs

RAs utilize the pillars and goals of the ANCHORS framework to develop thoughtful programs that encourage students to step outside of their comfort zone. Through entertaining activities and thought-provoking discussions, students learn to embrace diversity, sustainability, and consent. They also discover how to engage with their peers and community through socializing and volunteering. With each program, residents have an opportunity to further their understanding of society and themselves.

Meaningful Conversations

Throughout the semester, RAs frequently interact with residents. Whether it’s a one on one, lunch, or on the way to class, these seemingly insignificant conversations help residents develop a profound sense of belonging and comfort. These interactions allow residents to feel comfortable with RAs, and they open new doors to growth. At incidents, residents are more likely to listen and respond positively to RAs as they enforce policy to keep everyone safe. Residents also find it easier to reach out in times of need, whether its for academic assistance, mental health issues, or regarding roommate problems. This ensures residents feel supported and accepted.

Community Building

Through educational programs, meaningful conversations, door decorations, and a plethora of other activities, RAs foster community among residents. Programs give residents an opportunity to meet students outside of the classroom or their sport of choice. Door decorations also provide a chance for residents to know those people they live around. Additionally, they make the bland hallways homier. These activities create a supportive community that truly makes “Hurst is Home” ring true.

My Contributions

During the 2021-2022 academic year, I have helped develop a strong, supportive community in Warde Hall through programs, meaningful conversations, and community building. Out of the three, meaningful conversations have been the most impactful on my residents. From day one, I emphasized that my role as an RA was to be there for their academic, mental, and social needs. This helped my residents see me as more than just a policy enforcer. Instead, they see me as a resource and someone they can trust. At incidents, this has caused them to be much more understanding and reasonable, and they have been unafraid to reach out afterwards. Similarly, my programs and other community building activities, which can be seen here, have helped my residents better understand their emotional health, consent, the academic resources available to them on campus, diversity, and more! Each of these conversations and activities have sharpened my social abilities and increased my overall knowledge, and I plan to use everything I've learned to continue helping even more students next year!
